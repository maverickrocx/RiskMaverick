Who is a Risk Manager?
🎯
The Guardian of P&L
Risk managers protect the firm's financial health by identifying, measuring, and controlling exposures before they translate into losses. They sit at the intersection of trading, finance, and strategy.
⚖️
Independent Oversight
Operating as the "second line of defence," risk managers provide independent challenge to trading desks and business units — ensuring risk is taken deliberately, not accidentally.
📊
Quantitative Backbone
Fluent in VaR, CVaR, Greeks, scenario analysis, and stress testing. Risk managers translate complex financial positions into clear risk metrics for senior management.
🌍
Global Cross-Asset Expertise
From energy derivatives and FX to credit portfolios and interest rate books — the modern risk manager operates across asset classes, geographies, and regulatory regimes.

Risk Management Framework

The Enterprise Risk Management Cycle

A disciplined, repeating process that converts raw exposures into managed, limit-governed positions. Aligned with Basel IV, COSO ERM, and ISO 31000.

1
Identify
Catalogue all risk sources — market, credit, operational, liquidity. Use heat maps and risk registers.
2
Measure
Quantify exposures using VaR, CVaR, DV01, PD/LGD/EAD, Greeks, scenario P&L.
3
Monitor
Daily limit surveillance, breach escalation, real-time dashboards, KRI tracking.
4
Control
Set limits, approve new products, enforce hedging policy, manage concentrations.
5
Report
CRO dashboard, Board Risk Committee packs, regulatory submissions (FRTB, ICAAP).
6
Review
Backtesting, model validation, stress test outcomes, lessons from losses.

Value at Risk (VaR) — Key Methodologies

VaR(α,T) = μ − z_α · σ · √T
MethodApproachPros / Cons
Historical SimRe-price portfolio over 250 past daysNo distribution assumption / lags structural breaks
ParametricVariance-covariance matrixFast / assumes normality (underestimates tails)
Monte Carlo10,000+ simulated price pathsFlexible / computationally intensive
Expected ShortfallAverage loss beyond VaR thresholdBetter tail risk / Basel FRTB mandated

3. Rate Inherent RiskLikelihood × Impact before controlsBusiness
4. Map ControlsIdentify preventive and detective controlsBusiness
5. Rate Residual RiskLikelihood × Impact after controlsRisk Challenge
6. Action PlanFor red risks: remediation plan + ownerBusiness Lead
